Homemade Bread Stuffing

Description

Delicious and easy-to-make stuffing that is perfect for your Thanksgiving dinner

Ingredients

  • 10 cups dried bread cubes (white, sourdough, or a mix)
  • 1 ½ cups chopped yellow onion
  • 1 ½ cups chopped celery
  • 8 tablespoons (1 stick) unsalted butter
  • 2 ½ cups chicken broth (or vegetable broth)
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten (optional for richer stuffing)
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 ½ teaspoons dried sage
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • ½ teaspoon dried rosemary, crushed
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh parsley

Steps

  1. Prep the bread

    Spread the dried bread cubes into a large bowl. (If your bread isn't fully dried, toast cubes in a 300°F oven for 20-30 minutes until crisp.)

  2. Sauté the vegetables

    1. Melt the butter in a large skillet over medium heat.
    2. Add the onion and celery.
    3. Cook 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until soft and fragrant but not browned.

  3. Season

    Stir in the sage, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Let the spices bloom in the butter for about 30 seconds.

  4. Combine with bread

    Pour the sautéed vegetables and butter over the bread cubes. Add the chopped parsley. Mix gently until the bread is coated.

  5. Add broth (and eggs if using)

    Slowly pour in the broth, starting with 2 cups. Toss until the bread is evenly moistened but not soggy. If using eggs, stir them in now—they help bind the stuffing and make it richer.

  6. Transfer to a baking dish

    Spread the mixture into a greased 9 by 13 baking dish. Press lightly to even it out but don't pack too tightly.

  7. Bake

    Bake at 350°F (177°C) for 35-45 minutes, or until the top is lightly golden and the center is set.
